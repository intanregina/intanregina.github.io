03How I Work

Strategy work, done well, is not about decks. The decks are the artifact. The real work happens before the first slide — and continues after the deck is delivered.

STEP 01

Understand the commercial question

Before I look at any data, I clarify the question the client actually needs answered. "How is the campaign performing?" is rarely the real question. The real question is usually "should I renew, expand, or reallocate budget — and why?" I work backwards from that decision.

STEP 02

Interrogate the data

I work with raw measurement data directly — running my own analysis where I can, partnering with our data team for queries beyond my technical scope. I look for the number that tells the story, not just the numbers that fill a slide. If a metric looks surprising, I dig until I can explain it confidently.

STEP 03

Build the narrative

A senior audience will not read fifty slides. They will read the headline of each slide and the first sentence below it. So I write the deck the way an executive reads it: one provocative insight per slide, supported by the minimum data needed, leading to one clear recommendation.

STEP 04

Pressure-test for delivery

Because I also run sprints, I stress-test every recommendation against operational reality. Can the team actually build this? In what cycle? At what cost? The strategist in me builds the story; the Scrum Master in me makes sure the story holds up when engineering opens the ticket.

A major Indonesian FMCG conglomerate leads its category in national share of voice for OOH advertising — but holds 0% SOV in Jakarta, where its closest competitor runs 30+ uncontested placements. The client was dominating the map but losing the capital. They had won distribution — the next battle was winning moments.

The client responded strongly to the deck and opened follow-up conversations with two of their major sub-brands within the same group. Discussions ongoing.

Tobacco · East Jakarta Corridor

A quarterly business review that turned a credibility risk into the headline.

The Brief

An existing tobacco client needed a Q1 performance review across 14 OOH inventory units in the East Jakarta corridor. The deliverable needed to answer one commercial question: which assets to extend, which to relocate, and which to drop heading into Q2.

My Role

Owned the entire QBR end-to-end — from defining the measurement framework, to working with the data team on reach/frequency/overlap analysis, to writing the strategic recommendations, to building and presenting the client-facing deck.

The headline reach number looked initially like an over-counting error. A less-experienced analyst would have buried it. I led with it — and explained why it was a feature, not a bug.

The QBR strengthened client confidence in Playlog's measurement methodology and supported continued portfolio investment for Q2.

CASE 03
Telecommunications · National

A competitive defense for a market leader whose lead was thinner than it looked.

The Brief

A major Indonesian telecommunications operator held the #1 position in OOH share of voice — but by a margin of only 8 billboards. The client needed to understand the true fragility of its lead and define a placement strategy that protected market leadership against price-driven competition.

My Role

End-to-end strategic owner. I led the competitive industry analysis, worked with the data team to pull placement and spend data across 1,300+ billboards and five competing brands, identified strategic insights at category, brand, and city level, and built the executive deck.

The client was winning the visible scoreboard but losing critical moments. Market leadership in this category isn't held by ad volume — it's held by showing up at the right moment for the right consumer.
Outcome

The deck reframed the client's understanding of their own market position — shifting the internal conversation from "we are #1" to "we are #1, and here is exactly where that position is fragile and how we defend it."
